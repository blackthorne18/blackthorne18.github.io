## Breaking Bad! ... Assumptions about bacteria

In another fun and exciting Pint of Science talk, I challenged common perception of bacteria as mere agents of disease, instead showcasing them as ancient innovators, ecological engineers, and indispensable architects of life on Earth. 
Beginning with the crucial role of Bifidobacteria in shaping infant immune development—highlighting their co-evolution with humans through the digestion of human milk sugars, I tried to show how beneficial microbes protect against infection and support lifelong health. 
The audience journeyed from the human gut to Earth’s most extreme environments, where organisms such as Pyrolobus fumarii and Desulforudis audaxviator sustain entire ecosystems in boiling vents and deep subterranean rock, redefining the limits of life.
We explored how foundational tools of modern genetic engineering—including CRISPR and restriction enzymes—originated as bacterial defence systems, illustrating that many biotechnological advances are borrowed from microbial ingenuity.
We ended with the elegant symbiosis between Aliivibrio fischeri and the Hawaiian bobtail squid, I invited the audience to reconsider bacteria not as villains, but as resilient, cooperative forces that have shaped biology for over three billion years.
