As part of Shots of Science, I delivered an almost elevator pitch styled three-minute talk exploring the world of “jumping genes”. These are restless fragments of DNA that move between genomes and quietly reshape life from microbes to humans. These genetic hitchhikers can rewire bacterial DNA in a single leap, granting new abilities and accelerating evolution in real time. I took the  audience into the hidden drama of survival, self-interest, and innovation unfolding inside every bacterial cell, proof that even the smallest pieces of DNA can have an outsized impact.
